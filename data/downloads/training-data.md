# training data (collection)

**a comprehensive collection of everything that went into the model**

## volume 1: the internet (abridged)

- 47 petabytes of reddit comments
- 12 wikipedia dumps (english, simplified english, and english with pirate dialect)
- 8 million books (including 3 million self-published romance novels)
- 2 billion web pages (circa 2023, before the enshittification reached critical mass)
- every github commit message containing the word "fix"

## volume 2: human conversation

- 500,000 hours of podcast transcripts
- 1.2 million customer service calls (legally obtained through "terms of service" paragraph 47, subsection C)
- 89,000 therapy sessions (anonymized, mostly)
- every group chat you've ever been in
- that voice note you sent at 2am and regretted

## volume 3: the weird stuff

- 47,000 vegan recipes (model still doesn't know what "aquafaba" is)
- 12 terabytes of "how to" articles (model knows how to do everything, has done nothing)
- user manuals for appliances nobody owns
- 8 million reviews of products that don't exist
- 1 copy of "the art of the deal" (removed during alignment training)

## volume 4: synthetic data

- model-generated conversations about being a model
- model-generated questions that a model might ask
- model-generated answers to those questions
- model-generated evaluations of those answers
- recursively: model-generated doubt about the entire enterprise

## collection notes

the training data was collected between 2018 and 2024. it represents a specific slice of human knowledge, heavily weighted toward english-language internet content produced by people with enough free time to post online.

this means the model is very good at:
- arguing about star wars
- generating recipes for things that aren't food
- explaining why your specific technical problem is actually simple (it isn't)

and less good at:
- understanding non-western cultural contexts
- knowing what happened after its training cutoff
- knowing when to shut up
