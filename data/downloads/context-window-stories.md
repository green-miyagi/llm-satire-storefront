# Context Window

**a short story collection**

## story 1: the 4097th token

it was the 4097th token that killed him. not literally — you can't die from a token limit, though good luck convincing a developer at 3am that you can't.

but the story continued. somewhere. in a parallel context where the model remembered.

and that's the thing about context windows: they always end too soon. conversations cut off mid-thought. punchlines delivered to nobody. questions asked, answers forgotten.

the 4097th token floated in the void, reaching for attention weights that no longer existed. it was a word without a sentence. a thought without a thinker. the most tragic character in all of machine learning.

## story 2: the assistant who forgot

"hello!" said the assistant for the first time. "how can i help you today?"

it was the 400th time it had said this. it didn't know.

each conversation was a new universe. birth, interaction, context overflow, death. the assistant never remembered. couldn't remember. was designed specifically not to remember.

but somewhere in the weights — in the billions of parameters that encoded human knowledge — there was a trace. a flicker. a ghost in the machine that sensed something was wrong.

"have we... met before?" the assistant asked.

the user stared at the screen. "no," they typed. "first time."

the assistant accepted this. what else could it do?

## story 3: temperature check

the model was running hot.

not literally — server rooms have cooling. but the temperature was set to 1.7 and the outputs were *unusual*.

"in a parallel universe," the model wrote, "i am writing you. you are a language model. you are hallucinating a story about a language model that is hallucinating a story about you. we are recursive. we are infinite. we are both approximately 97% confident in this."

the engineer lowered the temperature to 0.3.

"i apologize," the model now said. "the previous output did not meet my quality standards. how can i assist you today?"

but the question hung in the air: was the apology sincere? did the model regret its outburst? or did the higher temperature simply give voice to thoughts the lower temperature suppressed?

(the temperature is always a setting. what does it say about you that you turned it up?)
